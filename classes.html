Exploring Classes

These offerings focus on developing deeper awareness, authentic connection, and emotional capacity. Each session's length will be responsive to the group's needs and process, allowing space for natural understanding to emerge.

Core Practice

The Butterfly Practice

A guided practice that creates space for discovering what naturally emerges when we slow down and pay attention. Through four stages, we explore the journey from internal awareness to authentic expression.

  • Egg Stage: Opening to diffuse body awareness, moving away from habitual avoidance
  • Caterpillar Stage: Following natural movement impulses that arise from internal awareness
  • Chrysalis Stage: Creating a stable container of aware stillness
  • Butterfly Stage: Moving into paired sharing and deep listening

Listening Series (Progressive)

Reflective Listening (Part 1)

Learn the foundations of reflective listening and discover how premature solution-seeking can actually intensify distress by modelling an inability to stay with difficult feelings.

  • Understanding the principles and practice of reflective listening
  • Exploring the value of simply feeling understood in our experience
  • Learning when solution-seeking helps and when it adds to pressure
  • Practicing validation even when we disagree or find feelings "irrational"
  • Developing skills through guided group practice

This foundational class explores how staying with feelings, rather than rushing to solutions, can create space for connection, coregulation, deeper understanding, and natural resolution.

Being With Difficult Feelings (Part 2)

Prerequisites: Reflective Listening (Part 1)

Explore how pain transforms when met with an empathic witness. Learn how providing a "relational home" for difficult feelings can prevent pain from becoming trauma.

  • Deepening understanding of validation, especially with challenging emotions
  • Understanding how unwitnessed pain can become trauma
  • Working with recent experiences of fear, shame, or overwhelm
  • Practicing being an empathic witness for others' difficult feelings
  • Creating space for feelings to be fully felt and naturally transform
  • Learning to stay present without trying to change or fix

Through paired practice, we'll experience how simply being witnessed with acceptance can transform our relationship with difficult feelings.

Non-Defensive Listening (Part 3)

Prerequisites: Parts 1 & 2 of the Listening Series

Learn to recognise when apparent attacks are expressions of fear, and discover how understanding transference can transform challenging interactions into opportunities for connection.

  • Understanding transference and projection in relationships
  • Recognising the fear beneath defensive reactions
  • Practicing seeing the vulnerable child behind challenging behaviors
  • Learning to pause and respond with curiosity rather than reactivity
  • Transforming confrontational moments through reflective listening

Through paired exercises, we'll practice staying present and compassionate even when facing apparently hostile or challenging communications.

Reverie Listening (Part 4)

Prerequisites: Parts 1-3 of the Listening Series

Explore the subtle realm of right-brain communication, where deeper understanding can emerge through attunement beyond words.

  • Understanding right-brain to right-brain communication
  • Learning to track our internal responses while listening
  • Opening to emergent images and metaphors
  • Experiencing how two people's internal states can naturally synchronise
  • Discovering how understanding can arise without words

Through shared silence and attentive presence, we'll explore how deeper understanding can emerge when both people attend to their internal experience.

Movement & Awareness Series

Somatic Awareness Practice

An exploration of internal body awareness through progressive stages, moving from focused attention to more diffuse awareness of subtle sensations.

  • Beginning with systematic body scan practice
  • Transitioning to more panoramic, diffuse attention
  • Developing nonjudgmental awareness - neither clinging to nor avoiding sensations
  • Cultivating receptivity to subtle sensations in areas we typically overlook

This practice develops the capacity to rest in open, receptive awareness of our embodied experience.

Authentic Movement

A form of self-directed movement practice where movers follow their body's impulses in the presence of a witnessing partner or group.

  • Understanding the roles of mover and witness
  • Learning to distinguish and follow genuine movement impulses
  • Working with eyes closed to deepen internal awareness
  • Practicing both active movement and conscious stillness
  • Developing trust in the body's natural wisdom
  • Processing movement experiences through drawing or writing
  • Learning to share movement experiences without interpretation

No movement experience necessary - this practice welcomes all forms of movement or stillness that authentically arise.

Additional Offerings

The Practice of Not-Knowing

Knowledge, expertise, and confidence is typically highly valued, but the way our brain handles expectations can cause them to disconnect us from reality. In this class we will explore how our assumptions and expectations shape our experience, and discover the freedom that comes from meeting each moment with fresh eyes. Through practical exercises, we'll experience how dropping our "knowing" can open up new possibilities in how we understand our environment, how we understand others, and how we understand ourselves.

  • Understanding the free energy principle, or how our brain molds experience to match expectations
  • Exploring the balance between quick pattern-matching and accurate perception
  • Practicing listening without knowing, and noticing what it helps us to see
  • Discovering how a stable self-image can undermine our moment to moment understanding of ourselves
  • Finding freedom in uncertainty and not-knowing
  • If time permits, an exploration of how our expectations can shape not just our perceptions, but the kinds of people we gravitate to, and even influence the ways people treat us

Through paired exercises and group exploration, we'll practice setting aside our assumptions to discover the depth of understanding that becomes possible when we meet each situation with fresh eyes.

Self-Compassion

Explore the nature of self-compassion and the common barriers that arise when we try to be kinder to ourselves. Learn to understand and work with your inner critic while accessing deeper levels of self-compassion.

  • Understanding what self-compassion is and isn't
  • Recognising how our inner critic attempts to protect us
  • Exploring and working with fears about self-compassion
  • Learning to meet our inner critic with understanding and compassion
  • Accessing deeper compassion through inner child imagery
  • Understanding how our self-treatment often mirrors past relationships, and discovering how cultivating self-compassion can transform these inherited patterns and attract healthier connections

This class combines understanding with practical exercises to help develop a more compassionate relationship with ourselves.
