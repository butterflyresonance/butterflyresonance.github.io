Framework

Three Ways of Understanding

Psychodynamic Insight

Reveals how we hold onto hurt in untouched parts of our mind, protecting ourselves through unconscious patterns and defenses

Somatic Understanding

Shows how we carry hurt in our body - through tension, restriction, and disconnect from our physical experience

Movement Wisdom

Offers ways to reconnect with our body through strength, mobility, balance, skill, and expressive movement

The Hidden Impact

When our conscious mind pushes something away, covering it with layers of fear - fear of judgement, punishment, or isolation - the impact extends beyond the original hurt. This protection seeps into:

  • Our body, through tension restriction, and disconnection
  • Our relationships, through the ways our hurt can be expressed unconsciously
  • Most fundamentally, our relationship with ourselves, as we lose touch with our emotional truth

A Natural Integration

Movement culture provides a unique bridge back to ourselves:

Coming Home

Through increased attention to internal sensations, we naturally unearth feelings we've turned away from

Building Safety

Developing strength, mobility, balance, and skill helps us feel safer in our body

Finding Joy

Expressive movement reconnects us with playful, vital parts we may have hidden from fear of judgement

The Path Forward

Somatic therapy already recognises these connections through:

  • Response completion movements
  • Grounding reflections
  • Release movements
  • Body awareness practices
  • Emotional integration work

Our vision is to deepen these connections, bringing together diverse approaches that touch this fundamental thread of human experience. Through collaboration between psychodynamic understanding, somatic awareness, and movement practice, we hope to create new pathways for authentic expression and healing.

For Practitioners

Creating collaborative spaces for integrated practice

We're building an inclusive community bringing together practitioners from diverse backgrounds to support authentic expression and freedom through movement and internal awareness.

  • Psychotherapy and somatic therapy
  • Movement and embodiment practices (yoga, dance, capoeira, martial arts)
  • Body awareness and meditation practices (breathwork, qigong, tai chi)
  • Athletic and acrobatic disciplines (calisthenics, gymnastics, acrobatics, parkour)
  • Expressive and performing arts
  • Contemplative and spiritual traditions (Sufism, Buddhism, Taoism)

If you're interested in our integrated approach to supporting authentic expression and embodied freedom, we'd love to connect with you.

Our vision is to create a space where diverse modalities come together to support true freedom - the freedom to feel all feelings, to embody all parts of ourselves, and to move in ways that authentically express who we are.
